About Me

I am a medical student at San Juan Bautista School of Medicine in Caguas, Puerto Rico, currently in my third year of training. As the daughter of Colombian immigrants, born in the United States and raised in Canada, I bring a unique multicultural perspective to my medical education and patient care.

My passion lies at the intersection of dermatology, research, and technology. I am particularly interested in how AI can be used beyond the doctor-patient interaction to improve the medical system and patient outcomes. Having completed clinical rotations in various specialties, I have developed a deep appreciation for culturally competent care ‚Äî something my trilingual fluency in English, Spanish, and French allows me to provide.

Outside medicine, I dedicate my time to volunteering at local clinics, participating in community health initiatives, and pursuing research in dermatology and public health. When I need a break, you'll find me playing soccer, going on long walks, drawing, swimming, or playing Dungeons and Dragons.
